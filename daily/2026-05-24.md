## [From Firefighting to Building: How AI Agents Restored Our Team's Core Productivity](https://engineering.grab.com/from-firefighting-to-building) 👨‍💼

Grab's Central Data Team automated data warehouse engineering support with a multi-agent system that separates incident investigation from remediation enhancement — a specialized investigation agent diagnoses root causes against warehouse state, an enhancement agent proposes and applies remediations, and an orchestration layer routes and coordinates between them. The architectural split creates a clean failure boundary: the enhancement agent only acts on problems the investigation agent has already characterized, which bounds blast radius and makes escalations observable. After deployment, the team shifted from reactive support firefighting to proactive platform engineering work. #ai #distributed #engineering #softwareengineering

## [How We Reduced Storage 10x While Handling 60+ Billion Records a Day](https://life.wongnai.com/how-we-reduced-storage-10x-while-handling-60-billion-records-a-day-0ed9b9783362) 🤓

Rebuilding observability across three business units (LINE MAN, Wongnai, FoodStory) on a single self-hosted ClickHouse cluster, LINE MAN Wongnai's Platform & SRE team hit 1.5 million rows/second peak ingest, compressed 143 TB of raw telemetry to 14 TB on disk (10x ratio), cut observability costs 53%, and eliminated trace sampling entirely — achieving 100% retention after years of sampling-induced blind spots. The gains follow directly from ClickHouse's columnar storage model: analytical queries reading a handful of columns across billions of rows pay I/O only for those columns, making row-oriented scans obsolete at this scale. Unifying logs, metrics, and traces in one OLAP-native store also eliminated the cross-tool correlation overhead of the previous Grafana Tempo stack. #db #devops #softwareengineering #cloud

## [How OpenAI delivers low-latency voice AI at scale](https://openai.com/index/delivering-low-latency-voice-ai-at-scale/) 🤓

WebRTC at global voice-AI scale breaks the conventional full-termination model: when one process owns both packet forwarding and session state (ICE negotiation, DTLS, SRTP), Kubernetes load balancers cannot rebalance connections without tearing them down. OpenAI's relay-transceiver split fixes this by placing a stateless Go relay in userspace near the user — reading packet headers and forwarding without terminating WebRTC — while a separate transceiver owns all stateful session machinery and scales independently. This decoupling separates the scaling curves of edge latency and session-state complexity: the relay distributes globally for fast connection setup and low RTT, the transceiver handles the expensive crypto and negotiation wherever session demands require. #distributed #architecture #softwareengineering #ai
