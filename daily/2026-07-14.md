## [Amazon SQS turns 20: Two decades of reliable messaging at scale](https://aws.amazon.com/blogs/aws/amazon-sqs-turns-20-two-decades-of-reliable-messaging-at-scale/) 👨‍💼

Twenty years after SQS launched on July 13, 2006 alongside EC2 and S3, the decoupling primitive at its core has remained unchanged while the scale envelope expanded substantially: FIFO high-throughput mode grew from 3,000 TPS per API action when it launched in May 2021 to 70,000 TPS in select Regions by November 2023, the in-flight message limit for FIFO queues tripled from 20,000 to 120,000, and max message size quadrupled from 256 KiB to 1 MiB. The same queue abstraction now buffers requests to large language models, manages inference throughput, and coordinates communication between autonomous AI agents operating as independent services — demonstrating how a well-scoped distributed primitive accretes new use cases rather than requiring architectural replacement over two decades. #distributed #architecture #cloud #softwareengineering

## [Introducing Precursor: detecting agentic behavior with continuous client-side signals](https://blog.cloudflare.com/introducing-precursor/) 👨‍💼

Bot traffic now generates 57% of all web requests and AI agents are capable of executing sophisticated multi-step browsing sessions indistinguishable from humans at the click level, rendering static CAPTCHA snapshots increasingly ineffective. Cloudflare's Precursor, now generally available, takes a fundamentally different approach: a compact dynamically injected client-side script monitors the full browser session — mouse trajectories, scroll velocity, typing cadence, and page visibility transitions — to assign a continuously updated Bot Score rather than a one-shot classifier result. The system records aggregate behavioral patterns instead of raw input, preserving user privacy while catching automation that passes isolated static checks; the design is deliberately agentic-first, built to detect the kinds of coordinated multi-step interactions that AI agents produce even when they partially mimic human behavior. #security #devops #architecture #softwareengineering
