## [Benchmarking Coding Agents on Databricks' Multi-Million Line Codebase](https://www.databricks.com/blog/benchmarking-coding-agents-databricks-multi-million-line-codebase) 👨‍💼

On Databricks' internal benchmark spanning a multi-million line codebase in Python, Go, TypeScript, and Scala, GLM 5.2 and Opus 4.8 land statistically tied on quality — but GLM costs $1.28/task against Opus's $1.94. The more striking finding is that token prices are a misleading proxy for total task cost: variance in reasoning efficiency across models means per-token pricing says little about what agentic coding loops will actually consume. The Pareto frontier (best quality per dollar) currently requires blending models from multiple providers — no single model dominates across both cost and quality dimensions simultaneously. #ai #llm #engineering #softwareengineering

## [The Agent Loop: How AI Goes From Answering Questions to Doing Things](https://blog.bytebytego.com/p/the-agent-loop-how-ai-goes-from-answering) 👨‍💼

Handing loop control to a model compounds errors multiplicatively: a 95% per-step success rate falls to 60% joint success over 10 steps and 36% over 20. PR-AF, an open-source code review agent, shows that harness design matters more than model choice — by planning a review strategy per PR, spawning parallel reviewer subagents, and verifying every finding against source before reporting, it ranks #2 of 42 on Martian's Code-Review-Bench ahead of CodeRabbit, Copilot, and Devin, at roughly 10× lower cost per review than closed-source tools. The architecture separates guardrails into three families by loop position: input guardrails (pre-model, catching policy violations and prompt injection), turn-level guardrails (mid-loop, blocking unsafe tool calls), and output guardrails (post-loop, catching leaked secrets or disallowed claims). #architecture #ai #softwareengineering

## [How Nubank Distributes Infrastructure Ownership to Operate More Than 21 Thousand Databases with a Lean Team](https://building.nubank.com/how-nubank-distributes-infrastructure-ownership-to-operate-more-than-21-thousand-databases-with-a-lean-team/) 👨‍💼

Nubank runs 21,000+ production databases across Brazil, Mexico, and Colombia maintained by just 5 dedicated database engineers — achieved not through heroics but through making database ownership a first-class product engineering responsibility. Every team that consumes a database also manages it via Nimbus, a declarative infrastructure repository where teams express desired state and automation enforces it, handling provisioning, patching, lifecycle transitions, and decommissioning without the platform team touching individual instances. Governance is baked into the system itself: the platform team owns the automation layer, not the databases, turning what is usually a centralized bottleneck into a shared operational commitment across hundreds of engineers. #db #architecture #devops #engineering
