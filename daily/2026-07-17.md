## [The State of Apache Polaris in July 2026: From Incubating Catalog to the Governance Layer of the Open Lakehouse](https://amdatalakehouse.substack.com/p/the-state-of-apache-polaris-in-july) 👨‍💼

Apache Polaris graduated from ASF incubation to a Top-Level Project in February 2026 and now ships on a monthly cadence, with RBAC, catalog federation, credential vending, Iceberg SQL views, and generic tables running in production across multi-engine lakehouses. A single Polaris instance can act as a "catalog of catalogs," federating external sources like AWS Glue and Hive Metastore without requiring a full migration — the practical on-ramp for organizations unwilling to rip out existing metastores. The most contentious open question heading into H2 is semantic layer ownership: a vote on a Polaris-native semantic model API was pulled to align with Apache Ossie, a freshly incubating semantics project, signaling that this layer of the open lakehouse stack is still genuinely unsettled. #db #architecture #cloud #softwareengineering

## [The State of Streaming to Apache Iceberg in July 2026: Every Path, Its Latency, and What to Do When Seconds Are Not Fast Enough](https://amdatalakehouse.substack.com/p/the-state-of-streaming-to-apache) 👨‍💼

The central question for Iceberg practitioners in mid-2026 is no longer "should we use Iceberg" but "how fresh can our tables be" — and the answer breaks into four latency bands: tuned Flink with aggressive checkpoints at 10–30 seconds, standard Flink or aggressive Spark at 30 seconds to 2 minutes, comfortable Spark/Kafka Connect/broker-native tables at 1–15 minutes, and sub-10-second freshness only through hybrid architectures where Iceberg anchors history while a streaming database (RisingWave, ksqlDB) serves live reads. Flink CDC is the standard bearer for near-real-time operational database mirroring, but it comes with maintaining a streaming pipeline as a first-class production system — not an ETL afterthought. The middle of the market, minutes-fresh tables fed from Kafka or CDC, is genuinely commoditized in 2026 with a dozen solid options spanning open source and managed services. #db #distributed #architecture #softwareengineering

## [Clustering Billions of Products for Agentic Commerce with Catalog API](https://shopify.engineering/catalog-clustering) 🤓

Shopify's Catalog clusters billions of product listings across millions of merchants with no shared schema by solving shop-level clustering first — treating cross-merchant deduplication as millions of independent per-store problems — then chaining into cross-store unification under a Universal Product Identifier (UPI). LLM-powered dynamic structured outputs convert product labeling from best-effort inference into a guaranteed constraint: every product gets a label, enabling deterministic chunking at any shop size and pushing precision high while continuously improving recall against calibrated LLM judges and refreshed ground truth. AI-powered searches built on Catalog data convert at 2× the rate of searches built from scraped data, making the underlying data quality investment directly measurable in commerce outcomes. #ai #architecture #softwareengineering #engineering
