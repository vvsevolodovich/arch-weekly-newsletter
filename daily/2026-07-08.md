## [State of AI Infrastructure Report Overview](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/compute/state-of-ai-infrastructure-report-overview/) 👨‍💼

A survey of 1,400+ senior IT leaders finds that 83% of organizations need infrastructure upgrades to run production-grade agentic AI, and 62% are already absorbing a measurable "inference tax" — data egress fees, storage bloat, and idle specialized hardware inflating total cost. Operational complexity is the hidden line item: 81% cite it as a significant cost driver, while consolidation around primary cloud partners is accelerating fast (78% now source gen AI directly from their primary cloud, up 30 points from 2025). Google frames the architectural response as five converging shifts: fluid compute matched to silicon type, centralized governance via Agent Gateway, unified data layers, hybrid/edge deployment, and purpose-built co-designed silicon — the new TPU 8t delivers 3× compute performance at 2× energy efficiency over the prior generation. #cloud #ai #architecture #engineering

## [Viability of local models for coding](https://martinfowler.com/articles/exploring-gen-ai/local-models-for-coding-factors.html) 🍼

Running local LLMs for agentic coding is a meaningfully different challenge from autocomplete: tool calling — the backbone of autonomous agent loops — remains brittle in small models, with frequent failures and only partial self-recovery, while output quality is "very hit and miss" compared to frontier cloud models. The hardware ceiling is hard: weights must fit entirely in available RAM/VRAM or the runtime crashes or degrades to unusably slow speeds, making GPU VRAM the primary gating constraint for viability at any given quality tier. Four weeks of hands-on testing (published July 7) finds small models appropriate for in-editor autocomplete but not yet reliable for fully autonomous agentic loops, and notes that tooling UX plays an outsized role — developers who don't want to wade through LLM specs need tools like LM Studio to have any realistic path to adoption. #llm #ai #development #engineering

## [Drive proactive security, prioritize risks with Google Threat Intelligence and Wiz ASM](https://cloud.google.com/blog/products/identity-security/drive-proactive-security-prioritize-risks-with-google-threat-intelligence-and-wiz-asm/) 👨‍💼

Traditional CTI (threat intelligence) and ASM (attack surface management) solve complementary problems but are typically siloed — this integration feeds Wiz's external exposure map (domains, IPs, APIs, exposed secrets, misconfigurations) directly into Google Threat Intelligence's adversary correlation engine, so prioritization reflects which of your exposed assets adversaries are actively targeting today, not theoretical severity scores. Wiz's Red Agent layer adds AI-driven scanning for logic-level vulnerabilities — authentication bypasses, multi-step attack chains, and business logic flaws — that signature-based scanners miss structurally. The combined architecture shifts security operations from a reactive patching queue to a live exposure-versus-adversary-behavior dashboard, giving defenders a prioritized action list rooted in real attack patterns rather than generic CVE rankings. #security #cloud #architecture #engineering
