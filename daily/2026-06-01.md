## [Databricks at SIGMOD 2026: Enzyme and Spark Declarative Pipelines](https://www.databricks.com/blog/databricks-sigmod-2026) 🤓

Enzyme is Databricks' incremental view maintenance (IVM) engine powering Spark Declarative Pipelines, receiving an honorable mention at SIGMOD 2026 (Bengaluru, May 31–June 5). Built atop Spark primitives, it adds a cost-based optimizer that selects per-collection refresh strategies across DAGs of materialized views — all IVM complexity is hidden from pipeline authors. Validation across thousands of large-scale production pipelines has produced a cumulative daily compute reduction of billions of CPU seconds. Databricks is also presenting the VLDB 2026 paper "A Decade of Apache Spark Structured Streaming: How We Evolved the Architecture To Meet Real-world Needs." #distributed #db #architecture #engineering

## [How NetEase Games Achieved 30-Second LLM Cold Starts on Kubernetes](https://www.cncf.io/blog/2026/05/21/how-netease-games-achieved-30-second-llm-cold-starts-on-kubernetes/) 👨‍💼

For 70B+ parameter models, bare Kubernetes cold starts at NetEase took up to 42 minutes — entirely dominated by pulling model weights from remote storage into GPU nodes. The fix was CNCF Fluid, which treats datasets and inference runtimes as first-class Kubernetes resources, enabling weight prefetching before pod startup, cross-namespace sharing of common base model weights, and schedule-aware warm-up for predictable traffic windows. Running on Tmax, NetEase's Kubernetes-native AI platform, this reduced cold-start time to 30 seconds — roughly an 84x improvement. #ai #cloud #devops #engineering

## [How Jaeger Is Evolving to Trace AI Agents with OpenTelemetry](https://www.cncf.io/blog/2026/05/26/how-jaeger-is-evolving-to-trace-ai-agents-with-opentelemetry/) 👨‍💼

Jaeger v2 rebuilt its core around the OpenTelemetry Collector, natively ingesting OTLP and eliminating legacy translation overhead — but the harder challenge is AI observability. Multi-step agentic workflows (tool calls, memory lookups, ephemeral sandbox executions) don't fit traditional span models, so Jaeger is tracking two active OTel drafts: one standardizing spans for Generative AI Agentic Systems (tasks, memory, actions) and one for AI Sandboxes. The project is simultaneously adopting MCP, ACP, and AG-UI protocols so engineers and AI systems can share the same trace context without vendor lock-in. #ai #engineering #architecture #softwareengineering
