## [How We Moved Discord Voice to the Edge](https://discord.com/blog/how-we-moved-discord-voice-to-the-edge) 🤓

More than 80% of Discord's voice and video traffic now runs on Cloudflare's edge network, with 70% of regions showing year-over-year quality improvements — but the migration surface area extended well past spinning up PoPs. The sharpest finding: adding coverage doesn't help if call placement logic is wrong. Iceland tests revealed that non-Iceland users landing on Iceland-hosted calls took a 2.7x ping increase and 9% higher packet loss, inverting the assumption that a closer PoP always wins for mixed-region calls. A subtler failure in multiple PoPs caused up to 25-second unresponsiveness: write-heavy voice workloads filled the page cache on newer disks until the kernel flushed several GB at once, stalling every other disk writer until it drained. Discord's answer was to stop chasing coverage gaps and instead replace existing regions one at a time, paired with smarter per-call host placement logic. #distributed #architecture #devops #engineering

## [Engineering the Forge Billing Platform for Reliability and Scale](https://www.atlassian.com/blog/atlassian-engineering/engineering-the-forge-billing-platform-for-reliability-and-scale) 👨‍💼

Atlassian's Forge billing platform processes roughly 300 million usage events per day with exactly-once guarantees — a hard requirement because missing or double-counting an event becomes a revenue or billing accuracy error, not just a data quality issue. The pipeline runs from Forge services (Atlassian's serverless extensibility runtime for Jira and Confluence apps) through a streaming layer into the Usage Tracking Service (UTS), which validates, normalizes, and enriches each event before handoff to commerce and invoicing systems. Idempotency is enforced end-to-end so any retry is safe; the deduplication and aggregation layer is the coordination point for events that can arrive out of order or more than once from distributed app instances. Billing went live January 1, 2026, with first developer invoices following in February — making this a practical blueprint for anyone instrumenting usage-based billing on a multi-tenant serverless platform. #architecture #distributed #engineering #softwareengineering

## [From Polyrepo Fragmentation to Monorepo Leverage](https://engineering.block.xyz/blog/from-polyrepo-fragmentation-to-monorepo-leverage) 👨‍💼

Block consolidated approximately 450 JVM repositories spanning Cash App and Square into a single monorepo, directly targeting a class of problems polyrepos make structurally expensive: dependency version drift, diamond dependency conflicts, and the coordination overhead of spreading a cross-service change across dozens of independent PRs and release cycles. The result supports around 8,800 weekly builds at roughly 10 minutes p90 CI time, enabled by dependency graph-based selective build execution that avoids rebuilding unaffected modules. The key architectural shift is that shared libraries no longer need their own versioned release cycle — the monorepo resolves them from source, eliminating the "is this library change deployed everywhere?" question entirely. A custom IDE plugin surfaces cross-service dependency impact at author time, catching integration issues before CI rather than after. #architecture #engineering #softwareengineering #development
