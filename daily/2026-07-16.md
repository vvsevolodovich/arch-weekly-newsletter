## [Modernizing the Meta Ads Service With an Open-Source Kernel Scheduler](https://engineering.fb.com/2026/07/13/ml-applications/modernizing-the-meta-ads-service-with-an-open-source-kernel-scheduler/) 🤓

When a Linux kernel upgrade risked regressing p99 latency across Meta's ads retrieval fleet (handling 5M+ requests per second), the team turned to sched_ext — a BPF-based extensible scheduling framework merged in Linux 6.12 that enables hot-swappable CPU schedulers at runtime without a reboot or kernel rebuild. Meta ported the LAVD scheduler originally designed for Valve's Steam Deck and extended it with ads-specific priority logic: work that reduces p99 request latency is scheduled first, everything else is deprioritized. The outcome: a 28% reduction in tail latency at the ads retrieval stage, 3.28 MW saved fleet-wide, and a 1.1% increase in ranked ads served — all from a scheduling policy deployed independently of the kernel upgrade. #distributed #architecture #engineering #softwareengineering

## [On-prem DBaaS in 2026: Platforms, standards, and gaps](https://www.cncf.io/blog/2026/07/15/on-prem-dbaas-in-2026-platforms-standards-and-gaps/) 👨‍💼

On-premises database provisioning in 2026 splits into two camps that rarely interoperate: developer-operated models built on Kubernetes operators (CloudNativePG, Vitess) and platform-team models backed by Crossplane or internal IDP tooling that abstract the infra layer. The critical gap is the absence of any widely adopted standard connecting database lifecycle management to the internal developer portal — leaving teams to rebuild equivalent abstractions repeatedly with incompatible APIs and operational assumptions. Until a common binding layer emerges, the tradeoff is real: tight operator integration with manual self-service wiring, or platform abstraction that substitutes cloud lock-in with a home-grown one. #db #architecture #devops #cloud

## [pgrust: Postgres Rewritten in Rust, Passing 100% of Regression Tests](https://malisper.me/pgrust-rebuilding-postgres-in-rust-with-ai/) 🤓

Malcolm Matis used eight parallel AI coding agents and 450,000+ lines of Rust to produce a full reimplementation of PostgreSQL 18.3 that passes all 46,066 regression queries with byte-for-byte identical output and boots directly from an existing Postgres 18.3 data directory. The architecture makes one immediate structural change: thread-per-connection replaces Postgres's process-per-connection model — the prerequisite for the next unpublished version's reported 50% better TPCC throughput and ~300× speedup on analytical workloads (2× slower than ClickHouse on ClickBench). The development method itself is the meta-story: c2rust mechanical translation followed by crate-by-crate auditing and rewriting with Claude is a concrete proof-of-concept for AI-assisted large-scale system rewrites, though v0.1 is explicitly not production-ready. #db #softwareengineering #development #engineering
