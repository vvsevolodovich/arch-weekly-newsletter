## [Meet Alice. Alice is impatient.](https://brooker.co.za/blog/2026/06/19/waiting.html) 🤓

Alice's mean wait time is 1 second even though the dashboard says the mean request takes 100ms — and both numbers are correct. The gap is the inspection paradox: customers experience a time-weighted version of your latency distribution, so what feels like "average" to Alice works out to E[X] + Var(X)/E[X], not E[X]. Brooker's argument: trimming or averaging away the slow tail erases exactly the experience that dominates how impatient users actually feel, which is why tail-latency work pays off more than median optimization. The piece drew 470+ points and 100+ comments on Hacker News. #distributed #architecture #engineering

## [Introducing Vercel Connect](https://vercel.com/blog/introducing-vercel-connect) 👨‍💼

Vercel Connect kills the stored-secret model for AI agents: instead of an API key sitting in an environment variable waiting to leak, your app presents the OIDC identity every Vercel deployment already has and gets back a short-lived, task-scoped credential at request time. It ships dedicated connectors for Slack, GitHub, Linear, Discord, Notion, Salesforce, Figma, and Snowflake, plus a generic OAuth 2.0/PKCE flow for any other service. The security win is structural, not procedural — there's no provider secret to commit, copy between environments, or rotate, because the agent re-requests access on every call instead of holding it indefinitely. A clean blueprint for scoping the blast radius of autonomous agents touching third-party APIs. #security #devops #cloud #ai

## [How Lightweight ADRs and Architectural Advice Forums Can Support Architectural Decisions](https://www.infoq.com/news/2026/06/architectural-decisions/) 🍼

Decentralizing architecture decisions works when you pair two lightweight habits: short, written Architecture Decision Records that force the deciding engineer to reason in public, and a regular open forum where anyone can offer advice or challenge a call before it's final. The combination produces an immutable, searchable log of why the system looks the way it does, doubling as onboarding material for new hires. No central architecture committee gatekeeps the decision; the ADR plus forum substitute peer review and trust-building for top-down approval. It's a practical pattern for teams that have outgrown a single architect but don't want decisions made in a vacuum either. #architecture #engineering #agile
